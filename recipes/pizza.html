Pizza



Ingredients

  • San Marzano Tomatoes
  • Type 00 flour
  • Fresh or active dry years
  • Salt
  • Mozzarella Cheese
  • Fresh Basil Leaves
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Optional Toppics (garlic, oregano, pepperoni)

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
    • In a separate container, dissolve the yeast in lukewarm water.
    • Slowly pour the yeast and water mixture into the flour while mixing. Knead the dough until it's smooth and elastic. It should be slightly sticky but not wet.
    • Divide the dough into smaller portions and shape them into balls.
    • Place the dough balls on a floured surface, cover them with a damp cloth, and let them rise for several hours at room temperature.

  2. Prepare the Pizza Sauce:
    • If you're using canned San Marzano tomatoes, simply crush them by hand and season with a pinch of salt. You can add a touch of olive oil for extra flavor.

  3. Heat the Oven:
    • Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can reach, typically around 500-550°F (260-290°C). Ensure the pizza stone or steel is inside while preheating.

  4. Shape the Pizza:
    • On a floured surface, gently flatten a dough ball with your hands, leaving a thicker border around the edge to form the characteristic Neapolitan crust.
    • Use your fingers to push the dough outward while rotating it, creating a thin center.

  5. Add Toppings:
    • Spread a small amount of the tomato sauce over the dough.
    • Tear fresh mozzarella into small pieces and distribute them on the pizza.
    • Add fresh basil leaves.
    • Drizzle a bit of extra virgin olive oil.
    • Sprinkle with a pinch of sea salt or kosher salt.

  6. Bake the Pizza:
    • Carefully transfer the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or steel in the oven. You may use a pizza peel or a flat baking sheet for this.
    • Bake for about 90 seconds to 2 minutes. The high temperature ensures a quick cooking time, creating the characteristic leopard-spotted crust.

  7. Serve:
    • Once the crust is puffed and charred, remove the pizza from the oven.
    • Let it cool for a moment, then slice and serve immediately.

Remember that Neapolitan pizza is all about simplicity and high-quality ingredients, and it's meant to be enjoyed right out of the oven when it's at its best.
